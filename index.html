Responsive Design

The primary device individuals use to browse the Internet is increasingly mobile or tablet. If your website isn't optimized for small screen devices, you are effectively cutting off an ever increasing portion of your audience.

Sources: Benedict Evans via Daring Fireball

Adding fuel to the fire, Google now adds mobile compatibility as apart of site rank score. Google does provide tools to check your site's mobile compatibility, but the benefits go beyond search engine ranking.

“[Google] will be expanding our use of mobile-friendliness as a ranking signal.”

Google Webmaster Central Blog "Finding more mobile-friendly search results"

Viewport vs. Device Widths

The Device Width is the width of the device, agnostic of the browser window and fixed as the physical device width.

The Viewport Width is the width of the browser window. On desktop, this can move around as the user adjusts the window size.

On Mobile devices, the viewport and device widths are the same… mostly.

URL Structure

  • Do not require URL parameters to view public content.
    • Incorrect: http://www.OfficalWebsite.com/?type=news&id=3942301
    • Correct: http://www.OfficalWebsite.com/news/release-date
  • Avoid language/country specific domain names.
    • www.spacecowboys.fr
      • Strengthens French speaking users.
      • But decrease in all other speaking users.
